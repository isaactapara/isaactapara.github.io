Port scanning is one of the first techniques used in cybersecurity reconnaissance. Before you can secure a system, you must understand what ports and services are exposed to the outside world.

This post introduces you to TCP scanning, why ports matter, and how ethical hackers use this technique to assess risk and identify vulnerabilities.

What is a Port?

A port is a logical access point for communication. Services like HTTP (port 80), SSH (port 22), and FTP (port 21) run on specific ports. If a port is open, it means a service is actively listening on that port.

TCP vs UDP Scanning

TCP scanning uses a full handshake process (SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK) to verify open ports. It's reliable and widely used. UDP scanning is stealthier but less reliable due to how it handles responses (or lack thereof).

Why Should You Care?

  • Identify exposed services
  • Understand your network surface
  • Detect shadow IT or misconfigured devices
  • Begin threat modeling with real data
